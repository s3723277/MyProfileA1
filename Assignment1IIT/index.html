Project Ideas

Overview: mtVNYL

Some people still cling onto their vinyl collections due to perhaps old age, and afraid of change. However, could it possibly be the fact that listening to a vinyl provides the listener with a better sound over its ever-growing streaming service competitor? That’s exactly right, to this day, people argue that vinyl provides better sound quality than a mp3 file due to lossless compression which is described as ‘a class of data compression algorithms that allows the original data to be perfectly reconstructed from the compressed data.’ So, what I’ve decided to come up with is to develop an app that scans the vinyl and plays it through a built-in record player on your smart device.

Motivation:

My motivation in regard to developing this project idea is that, like previously stated, a large amount of people still prefers vinyls over a streaming service, with ‘vinyls accounting to 12% of all album sales in the United States in 2018’ as stated by Statista. I’m also a fan of my vinyl collection, as I personally think having the physical evidence of an LP is something to boast about. Through the app, you’ll be able to take your beloved vinyls anyway you want and show off to anybody what your collection looks like. Through a free app, it won’t be like a streaming service where you have to pay a monthly fee, it will be provided free of charge. The only thing you would have to pay for are the vinyl’s which you already have!

Description:

So how the mtVNYL works is that, similarly to the app Shazam, I would scan the vinyl for an audio fingerprint however, often an acoustic fingerprint is a condensed digital summary, and where the predicament lies, is the fact that a vinyl produces an analog frequency. To cater for this, I will create a new database, unlike the one already developed for digital recordings, this will be entirely made up of analog recordings, to be able to distinguish between a vinyl recording and digital production of a song. So essentially, the vinyl database will be transferred into a digital signal, however, will maintain its analog integrity encapsulating the vinyl experience on the go. The idea of transferring your vinyl to your smart device is to maintain the vinyl integrity and sound quality, even after use, eliminating the prospect of wear and tear on the record. Often with vinyl records, they get exposed to worn out and damaged needles, which then damage the vinyl itself, and ultimately decrease life span of the record. What mtVNYL intends to achieve is protect and increase longevity of the vinyl itself, allowing for a longer lifespan than the proposed 1-5 years. Through importing your beloved collection of vinyls to your smart device, you’ll be able to have greater accessibility than ever before with your favourite hits on the go through increased portability. Along with the increased accessibility and portability, mtVNYL intends to eliminate the chance of distortion from weather or storage conditions. Vinyls lifespan and recordings are often tampered with when they encounter any weather conditions other than a dark cool room. As soon as they are exposed to light, they can become distorted and altered. This can also be seen happening when the needle becomes lost above the vinyl, often due to lack of pressure from the arm itself, causing the needle to jump to place to place.

Tools and Technologies:

A collection of technologies will need to be incorporated to produce the smart app mtVNYL. To begin, a software engineer will need to be established to create the bases and fundamentals of the app, delivering functionality. We will need to develop a new database to store all the vinyl recordings, along with a functioning operating system of iOS or Android. We would also need to include acoustic fingerprints, in order to trap and categorise the recordings, to then be able to relate it back to a spectrogram. Ultimately, we would also need a functioning hardware device such as a smart phone, which would be able to download the mtVNYL app.

Skills Required:

This goes hand in hand with the tools and technologies. In order to operate the tools we have access to, we have to be able to understand and acknowledge what it is we are using. As stated in the above paragraph, in order to get the app to function, we need someone who is adept in software engineering and is able to use a programming language which is best suited for the app. In this case, I have decided to continue with java, as it is universally known and has lots of functionality to it. To be able to understand databases, we need to ensure that we have the skills to acknowledge where data is being stored and maintained. Finally, we will need to be able to understand signal transmissions and frequencies, in order to comprehend acoustic fingerprints and the spectrogram. In terms of feasibility for this project, it definitely can be done, as there are so many similar projects these days, and the demand for app development is currently booming.

Outcome:

If the project were to be successful, I would encourage you vinyl lovers to download the app! Hopefully, the music industry will appreciate the development of the app, encouraging artists to continue to developing vinyl records of their albums. With the end goal of continuing to help artists grow and provide music lovers to experience music to the highest audio quality, whilst eliminating any opportunity for vinyls to have a decreased life span!
